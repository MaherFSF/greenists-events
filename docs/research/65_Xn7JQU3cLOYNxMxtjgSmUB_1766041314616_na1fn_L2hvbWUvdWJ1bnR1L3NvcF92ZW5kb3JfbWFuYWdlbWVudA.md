# Standard Operating Procedure: Vendor Management

**Company:** Greenists Event Management Platform
**Location:** Aden, Yemen
**Date:** 2025-12-18

## 1. Introduction

This document outlines the Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for vendor management for the Greenists Event Management Platform in Aden, Yemen. The purpose of this SOP is to establish a clear and consistent process for selecting, onboarding, managing, and paying vendors to ensure the highest quality of service for our clients and to maintain strong, professional relationships with our partners.

## 2. Vendor Management Process Overview

The vendor management lifecycle at Greenists is comprised of the following stages:

1.  **Vendor Identification and Sourcing:** Proactively identifying and sourcing potential vendors that align with Greenists' quality standards and client needs.
2.  **Vendor Qualification and Due Diligence:** Thoroughly vetting potential vendors to ensure they meet our legal, financial, and ethical standards.
3.  **Vendor Onboarding:** Integrating approved vendors into our platform and systems.
4.  **Engagement and Contracting:** Establishing clear terms of engagement and legally binding contracts.
5.  **Procurement and Service Delivery:** Managing the procurement of services and ensuring high-quality delivery.
6.  **Invoicing and Payment:** Ensuring a smooth and timely payment process.
7.  **Performance Management:** Continuously monitoring and evaluating vendor performance.
8.  **Vendor Offboarding:** Professionally managing the conclusion of vendor relationships.

## 3. Detailed Procedures

### 3.1. Vendor Identification and Sourcing

*   **Sourcing Channels:** Potential vendors will be identified through various channels, including:
    *   Online directories (e.g., Arabia Weddings)
    *   Industry recommendations and referrals
    *   Local market research
    *   Direct applications from vendors
*   **Initial Screening:** A preliminary screening will be conducted to assess the vendor's suitability based on their portfolio, experience, and online presence.

### 3.2. Vendor Qualification and Due Diligence

*   **Application:** Potential vendors must submit a formal application, including:
    *   Company profile
    *   Portfolio of work
    *   References
    *   Proof of legal registration and tax compliance
*   **Legal and Financial Verification:** Greenists will verify the vendor's legal status by checking their registration with the Ministry of Industry and Trade (MoIT). The vendor must provide a valid Tax Identification Number (TIN).
*   **Due Diligence Checklist:**
    *   Verify business registration documents.
    *   Check for a valid TIN.
    *   Review the vendor's financial stability (if necessary for large contracts).
    *   Conduct reference checks.

### 3.3. Vendor Onboarding

*   **Information Collection:** Once a vendor is approved, the following information will be collected and entered into the Greenists vendor database:
    *   Company Name (including Arabic name)
    *   Contact Person
    *   Address
    *   Phone Number
    *   WhatsApp Number
    *   Email Address
    *   Banking Information (for payments)
    *   Copy of legal and tax documents
*   **Platform Integration:** The vendor will be given access to the Greenists platform, where they can manage their profile, services, and bookings.

### 3.4. Engagement and Contracting

*   **Service Level Agreements (SLAs):** For each engagement, a clear SLA will be established, outlining the scope of work, deliverables, timelines, and quality standards.
*   **Contracts:** All engagements will be governed by a legally binding contract that includes:
    *   Names of both parties
    *   Scope of services
    *   Payment terms (including currency: YER and USD)
    *   Cancellation policy
    *   Dispute resolution mechanism

### 3.5. Procurement and Service Delivery

*   **Purchase Orders (POs):** For specific services, a PO will be issued to the vendor.
*   **Service Delivery Monitoring:** Greenists will monitor the vendor's performance throughout the service delivery process to ensure compliance with the SLA.

### 3.6. Invoicing and Payment

*   **Invoice Submission:** Vendors must submit a detailed invoice upon completion of the service, including:
    *   Invoice number and date
    *   Vendor's name and contact information
    *   Greenists' name and contact information
    *   Description of services rendered
    *   Total amount due (in YER and USD)
*   **Invoice Verification:** The finance department will verify the invoice against the PO and SLA.
*   **Payment Processing:** Approved invoices will be processed for payment within the agreed-upon timeframe. Payments will be made via bank transfer or other mutually agreed-upon methods.

### 3.7. Vendor Performance Management

*   **Performance Reviews:** Regular performance reviews will be conducted to assess vendor performance against key metrics, including quality, timeliness, and professionalism.
*   **Feedback:** Feedback will be provided to vendors to help them improve their services.

### 3.8. Vendor Offboarding

*   **Contract Conclusion:** When a contract ends, a formal offboarding process will be initiated.
*   **Final Payments:** All outstanding payments will be settled.
*   **System Deactivation:** The vendor's access to the Greenists platform will be deactivated.

## 4. Local Context: Aden, Yemen

*   **Business Registration:** All vendors must be legally registered in Yemen and possess a valid commercial registration.
*   **Taxation:** Vendors are responsible for their own tax obligations. The corporate tax rate in Yemen is 20%.
*   **Payment Methods:** While bank transfers are preferred, other payment methods such as Hawala or mobile money may be considered on a case-by-case basis.
*   **Communication:** WhatsApp is a primary mode of business communication in Yemen and should be used for timely updates and coordination.
