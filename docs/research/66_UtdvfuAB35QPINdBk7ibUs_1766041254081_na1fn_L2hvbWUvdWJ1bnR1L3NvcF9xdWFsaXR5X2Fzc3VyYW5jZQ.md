# Standard Operating Procedure: Quality Assurance - Inspection and Feedback

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1. Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the guidelines and procedures for quality assurance, inspection, and feedback for all events managed by the Greenists Event Management Platform in Aden, Yemen. The purpose of this SOP is to ensure that every event meets the highest standards of quality, exceeds client and guest expectations, and reinforces Greenists' reputation for excellence.

### 1.2. Scope

This SOP applies to all personnel involved in the planning, execution, and evaluation of events for Greenists, including event planners, coordinators, on-site staff, and management. It covers all stages of the event lifecycle, from pre-event planning to post-event evaluation.

### 1.3. Definition of Quality Assurance

For the purpose of this SOP, quality assurance (QA) is defined as the systematic process of ensuring that all aspects of an event are planned, executed, and evaluated to meet and exceed the expectations of clients, guests, and stakeholders. It involves a proactive approach to identifying and preventing potential issues, as well as a reactive process for addressing any problems that may arise. [1]

## 2. Pre-Event Quality Assurance

### 2.1. Venue and Supplier Inspection

A thorough inspection of all venues and suppliers is a critical first step in ensuring a high-quality event. This inspection should be conducted well in advance of the event date to allow for any necessary changes or adjustments. The following checklist, adapted from industry best practices, will be used for all venue and supplier inspections. [2]

#### 2.1.1. Venue Inspection Checklist

**Basic Information**

*   Venue Name (and Arabic Name if applicable)
*   Venue Type
*   Address
*   Contact Information (including WhatsApp number)
*   Date of Inspection

**Site Specifics**

*   Neighborhood and accessibility
*   Building appearance and landscaping
*   ADA compliance
*   Cancellation and attrition policies
*   Deposit and payment schedules (in YER and USD)

**Event Logistics**

*   Proximity to airport and hotels
*   Parking availability and fees
*   Valet parking options
*   Accessibility via public transportation
*   Directional signage

**Food & Beverage**

*   Catering options and menus
*   Pricing for various meal types (in YER and USD)
*   Service charges and taxes
*   Guarantees and overset policies
*   Food presentation, selection, and creativity

**Audio & Visual**

*   Available equipment and quality
*   Rental and labor rates (in YER and USD)
*   Union rules and requirements

**Meeting Rooms**

*   Room cleanliness and decor
*   Soundproofing and lighting
*   Temperature control
*   Proximity to restrooms

**Hotel Accommodations (if applicable)**

*   Room rates and availability
*   Complimentary room policies
*   Room cleanliness and amenities

### 2.2. Pre-Event Briefing

A pre-event briefing will be held with all staff and suppliers to review the event plan, timelines, and quality standards. This briefing will ensure that everyone is aligned on their roles and responsibilities and understands the expectations for the event.

## 3. During-Event Quality Assurance

### 3.1. On-Site Monitoring

Continuous on-site monitoring is essential to ensure that the event is running smoothly and that any issues are addressed promptly. A designated Quality Assurance Manager will be on-site for the duration of the event to oversee all aspects of the operation.

### 3.2. Real-Time Feedback

Mechanisms will be in place to gather real-time feedback from clients and guests during the event. This can include:

*   Regular check-ins with the client.
*   Comment cards or a digital feedback form.
*   A designated on-site contact person for any questions or concerns.

## 4. Post-Event Feedback and Evaluation

### 4.1. Post-Event Debriefing

A post-event debriefing will be held with all staff and suppliers to review the event and identify any areas for improvement. This debriefing will cover both successes and challenges and will be used to inform future event planning.

### 4.2. Client and Guest Feedback

Formal feedback will be solicited from the client and guests through a post-event survey. This survey will be designed to gather detailed feedback on all aspects of the event, including:

*   Overall satisfaction.
*   Venue and catering.
*   Staff and service.
*   Audio-visual and other technical aspects.
*   Value for money.

### 4.3. Data Analysis and Reporting

The feedback gathered from the post-event surveys will be analyzed to identify trends and areas for improvement. A post-event report will be prepared for the client, summarizing the feedback and outlining any actions that will be taken in response.
