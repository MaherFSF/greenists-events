## Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) for Kids' Event Planning

This section outlines a standardized workflow for planning and executing a successful children's event from conception to post-event analysis. These procedures are designed to ensure a consistent and high-quality experience for both the clients and the children attending the events.

### Phase 1: Initial Client Consultation and Requirement Gathering

1.  **Initial Contact:** Acknowledge the client's inquiry within 24 hours.
2.  **Consultation Meeting:** Schedule a meeting (in-person or virtual) to understand the client's vision, budget, and requirements.
3.  **Requirement Elicitation:** Use a standardized questionnaire to gather key information, including:
    *   Event type (birthday, graduation, etc.)
    *   Date and time
    *   Number of guests (children and adults)
    *   Age range of children
    *   Desired theme
    *   Venue preferences
    *   Catering requirements
    *   Entertainment preferences
    *   Budget constraints

### Phase 2: Proposal and Contract

1.  **Proposal Development:** Based on the gathered requirements, create a detailed event proposal, including:
    *   Event concept and theme
    *   Venue options
    *   Catering menus
    *   Entertainment options
    *   Detailed budget breakdown (in YER and USD)
    *   Timeline
2.  **Proposal Presentation:** Present the proposal to the client and make any necessary revisions.
3.  **Contract:** Once the proposal is approved, draft a formal contract outlining the agreed-upon services, payment schedule, and cancellation policy.

### Phase 3: Event Planning and Coordination

1.  **Venue Booking:** Secure the chosen venue and confirm all arrangements.
2.  **Supplier Coordination:** Book and coordinate with all necessary suppliers, including caterers, entertainers, decorators, and photographers.
3.  **Guest Management:** Send out invitations and manage RSVPs.
4.  **Event Schedule:** Create a detailed event schedule, including setup, activities, and breakdown.

### Phase 4: Event Execution

1.  **On-site Management:** An event manager should be present on the day of the event to oversee all activities and ensure everything runs smoothly.
2.  **Supplier Management:** Coordinate with all suppliers on the day of the event to ensure they are on time and providing the agreed-upon services.
3.  **Guest Assistance:** Provide assistance to guests and ensure they have a positive experience.

### Phase 5: Post-Event Activities

1.  **Post-Event Cleanup:** Oversee the cleanup of the venue.
2.  **Supplier Payments:** Ensure all suppliers are paid in a timely manner.
3.  **Client Feedback:** Solicit feedback from the client to identify areas for improvement.
4.  **Post-Event Analysis:** Conduct a post-event analysis to evaluate the success of the event and identify any lessons learned.
